Beck Depression Inventory BDI-II – Self-Rating Depression Severity Scale The Beck Depression Inventory is a self-assessment scale for determining the severity of depression, which consists of 21 items (these are at the same time individual symptoms and negative thoughts occurring to varying degrees in individuals with depression – sadness, pessimism, past failures, loss of joy, feelings of guilt, feeling punished, disgusted with oneself, self-criticism, suicidal thoughts or wishes, tearfulness, agitation, loss of interest, indecisiveness, feeling worthless, loss of energy, change in sleep, irritability, changes in appetite, concentration, fatigue, loss of interest in sex) . The Beck Depression Rating Scale is a questionnaire that is evaluated 1-2x per week. It shows the overall level of depression. You can compare for yourself how the intensity of depression changes over time and its individual symptoms.

Instructions:

The BDI-II questionnaire can be applied to persons from 13 years of age. First, read all the statements in a certain group and mark the one that best describes you. If you think both options apply, choose the one that suits you better, or on the contrary, skip the one that doesn't suit you more in the last 7-14 days. The result will show your depression severity score, depression severity, cognitive-affective depression symptom subscale, somatic-performance depression symptom subscale, and individual depression symptoms.
